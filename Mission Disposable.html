Pie graph that shows people who learned something from our workshop


In our summary, we tallied the responses of our participants about the effectiveness of our workshop. For question no.1, with the question Did you learn anything from our workshop? If yes, tell us what you learned. We got 8/8 or 100% of our participants said that they have learned something from the workshop. For question no.2, they were asked if they learned what they expected to learn from our workshop, 8/8 or 100% of our participants answered yes, they have learned what they expected to learn. In question no.3, We asked them if they think that the workshop will be helpful for the environment, 7/8 or 87.5% of our participants answered yes, and or 12.5 answered maybe. In question no.4, 7/8 or 87.5% answered Is effective, while 1/8 or 12.5% answered Maybe It Is, referring to the question What do you think about our workshop? Is it effective or not?. For the question no.5, we asked them about their suggestions they can give us to further improve our workshop, 1/8 or 12.5% answered No Problem, also 1/8 or 12.5 answered Others, 3/8 or 37.50% said that we need more explaining and also 3/8 or 37.50% of our participants said that we need more activities. For question no.6, we asked that on a scale of 1 to 5, how would you rate our workshop, 4/8 or 50% of our participants rated 5, on the other hand 4/8 or 50% rated us 4. And last question, question no.7, we asked if they would recommend the workshop to others, 6/8 or 75% answered yes, while 2/8 or 25% of our participants answered maybe.

Our Goals:
1. To know if our workshop is effective.
2. To identify errors and make them right.
3. To be able to know if we can deliver what we want to our participants
Methodology:
We conducted a dry run for our workshop named "Mission Disposable" which generally helps the people who participate in knowing proper waste disposal techniques. Eight chosen participants were given a chance to partake in this workshop. They participated in various activities and various discussions about the workshop. We made a very fun introduction to the workshop in order to make a good first-impression to them and also give them a chance to make suggestions for our workshop. After the session we asked them to answer a survey indicating if they learned something and if they have suggestions about the workshop in order to further improve ourselves in conducting it. We also took note of various issues about the workshop and took some time to analyze their answers and feedback to better fit our workshop.
Participants: 9AM-3

Findings
We found that 100% of our participants agree that our workshop is effective and that they learned from it. The total time spent for the 1st session of the workshop is 35 to 45 minutes. All of our participants are satisfied with the completion of the workshop, they also let comments on what to improve on for the workshop like, Improvement on speaking skills and to add more activities so it could be more interactive.
